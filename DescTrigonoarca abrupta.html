Trigonoarca abrupta" refers to a species of fossil bivalve belonging to the family Trigoniidae.
It is characterized by its trigonal (wedge-shaped) shell and the presence of abrupt, strong ribs on the flank.
The term "abrupta" likely refers to the abrupt or sharply defined nature of these ribs.

Fossils of Trigonoarca abrupta can be found in various locations and geological formations,
providing insights into past marine environments and the evolution of bivalves.
