Description

Name: Gongrochanus - Echinoidea
Formation: Ottakovil and Kallankurichchi Formations of Ariyalur Group
Locality: Kallankurichchi
Classification: Echinodermata - Echinoidea - Family: Fujasidae Lambert Genus: Congrochanus kier
Age: approximately 62 to 72 million years ago.

The specimens of Gongrochanus described here come from the
Ottakovil Formation of Ariyalur Group.
Recently a few specimens of this genus have also been collected from the
Kallankurichchi Formation of the same Group from near Kallankurichchi. Ref: 01.

Gongrochanus is a fossil genus of cassiduloid echinoids (sea urchins) from the Late Jurassic
and Cretaceous periods, known from specimens found in South America and India.
These ancient sea urchins belonged to the class Echinoidea, which also includes
modern sea urchins, but Gongrochanus represents a now-extinct lineage.

Fossil Genus: Gongrochanus is a fossil group, meaning it is only known from
preserved remains, not living creatures.

Cassiduloid: It belongs to the cassiduloid group of echinoids, which are a type of
irregular sea urchin characterized by their generally flattened, heart-shaped, or
oval bodies and sometimes possessing distinct frontal ambulacra.

Fossil Record: Species of Gongrochanus have been identified in Late Jurassic and
Late Cretaceous deposits, indicating a long history of existence.

Geographic Distribution: Fossils of Gongrochanus have been found in both
South America (Argentina) and India.

Echinoderms: As an echinoid, Gongrochanus is an echinoderm, a group of marine
invertebrates that also includes starfish, sea cucumbers, and sand dollars.
