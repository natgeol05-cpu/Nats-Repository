This is anterior caudal vertebra ispear-shaped in mesio-distal orientation.
The centra is acompressed amphicoelous disc suggesting its ichthyosauraffinity.
The posterior and anterior surfaces are smooth,excavated, sloping initially gently and
steeply from midwayto the center. Ventrally on the lateral surface, a rather slightly
raised facet showing a narrow linear extension dorsally forabout 1 cm is present.
This feature is seen on both sides ofthe centrum.

Ichthyosaurs were stream-lined marine reptiles that ranged in size from
7-30 feet long (4.5-9 m).
They had sharp teeth in long jaws, and big eyes.
They had four crescent-shaped fins, a stabilizing dorsal fin, and a fish-like tail with two lobes.
They breathed air with lungs through nostrils which were close to the eyes near the top of the snout.

Ichthyosauria[a] is an order of large extinct marine reptiles sometimes referred to
as "ichthyosaurs", although the term is also used for wider clades in which the order resides.

Ichthyosaurians thrived during much of the Mesozoic era; based on fossil evidence,
they first appeared around 250 million years ago (Ma) and at least one species survived
until about 90 million years ago,[2][3] into the Late Cretaceous.
