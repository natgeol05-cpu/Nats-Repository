Description

Name: Calycoceras asiaticum
Formation: Uttatur Group in Karai Formation
Locality: Kunnum dry reverbed
Classification: Mollusca - Cephalopada -Ammonitida - Genus: Calycoceras
Age: Temporal Range: 100 to 94 million years ago

Calycoceras is an extinct genus of cephalopods belonging to the subclass Ammonoidea
and family Acanthoceratidae that lived during the Cenomanian stage of
the Late Cretaceous, 100-94 Mya. Their shells had ornate ribs.

Shells of Calycoceras asiaticum may reach a diameter of about 20 centimetres (7.9 in).
The larger phragmocones may reach 59–72 millimetres (2.3–2.8 in) in diameter.
Coiling is moderately involute. All ribs show strong subspinose tubercles.
C. asiaticum asiaticum and C. asiaticum spinosum are very similar,
the latter has much more robust tuberculation.

One of the well-documented geological units in the southern Indian Cretaceous is
the Calycoceras newboldi Zone, which is found in the Ariyalur region.
This suggests that other species of the genus Calycoceras, including C. aisatum,
might also be present in the Ariyalur area, as the genus is known to have lived
during the time represented by the sediments found there.
