Description

Name: Fossil Wood
Formation: in red clay of Karai 'Badland''
Locality: Sathanur, Perambalur district, Tamilnadu, India
Classification: Single Cone coniferous

Ariyalur in Tamil Nadu, India, is known for its rich fossil deposits, including fossilized wood
dating back to the Cretaceous period. This area, once submerged under the sea,
reveals a wealth of ancient life, with fossilized wood found alongside other marine fossils.
The National Fossil Wood Park in nearby Sathanur, Perambalur district, is a key location
for viewing these fossilized trees, some over 18 meters long. (images below).

The fossilized wood found here is primarily coniferous, remnants of a time when these trees
dominated the land. The Sathanur Petrified Tree Educational Center includes a museum
with displays of fossils and information about the region's geological history.

In Karai Badlands, the red clays are abound in fossilwood.
This outcrop continues in eastern direction for about 2 km up to Kulakkanattam.

The petrified tree trunk is believed to be over 120 million years old, and is considered
to be evidence for the presence of a sea during the Cretaceous period in this area.
This fossil is of a conifer, measures around 18 meters long and a geological treasure.
Similar fossilized tree trunks have been found in nearby Varagur, Anaipadi, Alundalippur,
and Saradamangalam,[5] all located within 10 kilometres (6.2 mi) of Sathanur.
