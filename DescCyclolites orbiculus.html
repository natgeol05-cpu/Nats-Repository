Description

Name: Cyclolites orbiculus - corals.
Formation: Kallamedu/Ottakovil formation of Ariyalur Group
Locality: Kallamedu and Ottakovil
Classification: Cnidaria- Hexacorallia - Order: Scieractinia Genus: Cycloseris
Age: Range: from 100.5 to 66 million years ago

Cycloseris cyclolites is a species of disc coral in the family Fungiidae.
It was first described by the French naturalist Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1815.
It is native to the tropical and subtropical Indo-Pacific region where it is found on
soft sediment in shallow water.

Coral fossils are found in Kallamedu/Ottakovil formation of Ariyalur Group.
The age of the fossil coral Cycloseris cyclolites orbiculus dates back to
the Upper Cretaceous Period, approximately 66 to 100.5 million years ago.

A solitary disc coral, C. cyclolites is round or slightly oval, forming a dome up to
4 cm (1.6 in) wide with a hollowed out under surface.
The primary septa are thick and straight with up to four more whorls of septa in larger specimens.

Lifestyle:
Cycloseris cyclolites is found in oceans of the tropical and subtropical Indo-Pacific region.
Its range extends from Madagascar, the East African coast and the Red Sea through India and
Malaysia to northern Australia, Japan and various island groups in the Pacific Ocean.
Its typical habitat is on soft substrate, often muddy sand, between reefs, but it also
sometimes occurs on the reefs themselves.
